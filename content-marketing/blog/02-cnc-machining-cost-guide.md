## Introduction

"What should CNC machined parts cost?" It's the question every purchasing manager asks — and the answer is always "it depends." CNC machining quotes can vary 30-50% between suppliers for the same part, and understanding why is your key to better pricing.

This guide breaks down the real cost drivers behind CNC machining in 2026, with actual numbers you can use to evaluate quotes and negotiate better deals.

## The 5 Components of a CNC Machining Quote

### 1. Material Cost (15-30% of total)

| Material | Price/kg (approx.) | Machinability |
|----------|-------------------|---------------|
| Aluminum 6061 | $3-5 | Excellent |
| Steel 1045 | $2-4 | Good |
| Stainless 304/316 | $4-8 | Moderate |
| Brass | $6-10 | Excellent |
| Titanium Ti-6Al-4V | $25-40 | Difficult |

**Buy-to-machine ratio**: Raw material cost × 1.5-5x depending on how much material is removed. A part machined from a 10kg billet that weighs 2kg finished has a 5:1 ratio.

### 2. Machine Time (40-60% of total)

| Machine Type | Hourly Rate (USD) | Best For |
|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| 3-axis VMC | $75-125 | Prismatic parts, simple geometries |
| 5-axis VMC | $125-200 | Complex geometries, multi-sided parts |
| CNC Lathe | $60-100 | Round/cylindrical parts |
| Swiss-type | $100-175 | Small, precise turned parts |
| Grinding | $80-150 | Ultra-precision surfaces |

**The math**: Cycle time × hourly rate = machine cost per part. A 15-minute cycle on a 3-axis VMC at $100/hr = $25/part in machine time.

### 3. Setup & Fixturing (5-15% of total)

One-time cost per batch. Spread across more parts, setup cost per part drops significantly:
- 10 parts: setup might be $5/part
- 100 parts: same setup is $0.50/part
- 1000 parts: $0.05/part

**This is why MOQ matters.**

### 4. Post-Processing (5-15% of total)

| Process | Cost/Part (approx.) |
|---------|-------------------|
| Anodizing (aluminum) | $3-8 |
| Zinc plating | $2-5 |
| Powder coating | $5-15 |
| Heat treatment | $5-20 |
| Passivation (stainless) | $2-5 |

### 5. Quality & Inspection (5-10% of total)

- Standard dimensional report: included
- CMM inspection report: $5-20/part
- First Article Inspection (FAI): $50-200 one-time
- Material certification: $10-25/batch

## 7 Strategies to Reduce CNC Machining Costs

### 1. Design for Manufacturing (DFM)
- Avoid unnecessarily tight tolerances (see our [tolerance guide])
- Use standard hole sizes and thread pitches
- Minimize setup changes (design parts that can be machined in 1-2 setups)
- **Potential savings: 15-25%**

### 2. Optimize Material Selection
- Don't over-specify material grade if a standard grade works
- Consider near-net-shape (casting/forging) for large volume parts
- **Potential savings: 10-20%**

### 3. Increase Order Quantity
- Setup costs amortize over more parts
- Material purchasing gets volume discounts
- **100 pcs vs 10 pcs: typically 30-40% savings per part**

### 4. Consolidate Parts
- Can two parts be combined into one? Eliminates assembly and one setup
- **Potential savings: 20-40%**

### 5. Choose the Right Supplier Location
- US/EU machine shops: $75-150/hr
- China (quality shops): $25-50/hr
- **Same quality standard, 40-60% cost advantage**

### 6. Standardize Surface Finishes
- As-machined (Ra 1.6-3.2μm) is often sufficient
- Only add anodizing/plating where corrosion or wear resistance is needed
- **Potential savings: 5-15%**

### 7. Build Long-term Relationships
- Repeat orders = no re-setup cost for fixtures
- Suppliers offer better pricing for predictable volume
- Consignment inventory options reduce your carrying cost
