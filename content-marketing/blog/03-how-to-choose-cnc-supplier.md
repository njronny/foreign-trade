## Introduction

Choosing the wrong CNC machining supplier doesn't just cost you money — it costs you time, quality, and credibility with your own customers. We've seen it all: parts that don't fit, shipments that arrive weeks late, and quotes that mysteriously double after the first order.

After 10+ years serving North American clients, we've compiled this checklist from both sides of the table. Use it to evaluate any CNC supplier — including us.

## Quality System (Non-Negotiable)

### ☐ 1. ISO 9001 Certification (Minimum)
ISO 9001 is table stakes. If a shop doesn't have it, walk away. But also ask:
- When was their last audit?
- Do they have any non-conformances?
- Is their certificate current and from a reputable registrar?

### ☐ 2. Industry-Specific Certifications (If Required)
- Aerospace: AS9100, Nadcap
- Automotive: IATF 16949
- Medical: ISO 13485, FDA registration
- Defense: ITAR registration

### ☐ 3. Inspection Capabilities
A supplier worth working with should have:
- Calibrated measuring instruments (calipers, micrometers)
- CMM (Coordinate Measuring Machine) for complex parts
- Surface roughness tester
- Hardness tester
- Ability to provide dimensional inspection reports with every shipment

## Technical Capabilities

### ☐ 4. Machine Fleet
Ask for a machine list with:
- Machine type (3-axis, 4-axis, 5-axis, lathe, Swiss)
- Work envelope (max part size)
- Machine age and brand (newer = better capability)

### ☐ 5. Material Range
Can they machine the materials you need?
- Aluminum alloys (6061, 7075)
- Steels (mild, alloy, tool steel)
- Stainless steels (304, 316, 17-4PH)
- Specialty alloys (Inconel, titanium, Hastelloy)
- Engineering plastics (Delrin, Nylon, PEEK)

### ☐ 6. Tolerance Capability
Standard shops: ±0.01-0.05mm
Precision shops: ±0.005mm consistently
Ultra-precision: ±0.002mm or better

**Ask for tolerance capability data, not claims.**

### ☐ 7. Surface Finish Capability
- As-machined: Ra 1.6-3.2μm
- Fine finish: Ra 0.4-1.6μm
- Ground finish: Ra 0.1-0.4μm
- Post-processing: anodizing, plating, painting, heat treatment

## Communication & Service

### ☐ 8. English Communication
- Do they have English-speaking project managers?
- How quickly do they respond to emails? (< 12 hours = good)
- Can they discuss technical issues in detail?

### ☐ 9. Engineering Support
- Do they offer DFM (Design for Manufacturing) feedback?
- Can they suggest cost-saving design modifications?
- Do they review drawings before quoting?

### ☐ 10. Project Management
- Is there a single point of contact?
- Do they provide regular production updates?
- How do they handle engineering changes?

## Pricing & Terms

### ☐ 11. Quote Transparency
**Red flag**: A single line-item price with no breakdown.
**Good sign**: Itemized quote showing material, machining, setup, and finishing costs separately.

### ☐ 12. Payment Terms
- New supplier: expect 50% deposit + 50% before shipment
- Established relationship: Net 30 terms possible
- **Never pay 100% upfront**

### ☐ 13. Shipping & Logistics
- Do they offer DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)?
- Which carriers do they use? (DHL, FedEx, air freight, sea)
- Can they handle customs documentation?

## Track Record

### ☐ 14. References & Case Studies
- Can they provide customer references (even anonymized)?
- Do they have documented case studies?
- How long have they served international clients?

### ☐ 15. Trial Order Process
A confident supplier will:
- Accept a small trial order (5-10 pieces)
- Provide first article samples for approval
- Not require a large commitment upfront

## 7 Red Flags 🚩

1. **No ISO certification** — Walk away immediately
2. **Quote significantly lower than others** — You'll pay later in quality problems
3. **No inspection equipment** — They're guessing, not measuring
4. **Slow response time** (> 48 hours) — It won't get better after you place an order
5. **Won't accept trial orders** — They're afraid you'll see the quality
6. **No clear point of contact** — You'll be passed around
7. **Vague about lead times** — "About 2 weeks" means 4 weeks
