## Day 1 (周一): 行业洞察帖

Most CNC machining quotes vary by 30-50% for the same part.

Why?

It's not because someone is overcharging. It's because the total cost of a machined part includes:

❌ Quoted price
❌ Scrap rate cost
❌ Communication overhead
❌ Lead time risk
❌ Rework cost

The cheapest quote is rarely the cheapest outcome.

Next time you evaluate a CNC supplier, ask for an itemized quote. If they can't break down material, machine time, setup, and inspection costs separately — that's a red flag.

## Day 3 (周三): 技术知识帖

Quick comparison — Standard vs Precision CNC tolerances:

Standard (±0.1mm):
→ General brackets, covers, housings
→ Cost: baseline

Fine (±0.05mm):
→ Machine components, mounting plates
→ Cost: +20-30%

Precision (±0.01mm):
→ Hydraulic bores, gear housings
→ Cost: +50-80%

Ultra (±0.005mm):
→ Aerospace, medical, sealing surfaces
→ Cost: +100-200%

Pro tip: Only 10-20% of features on a typical part actually need precision tolerances. Spec tight only where it matters and you'll save significantly.

## Day 4 (周四): 客户反馈帖

A US hydraulic equipment company came to us with a problem:

"Their current supplier was delivering 15% defect rate on valve bodies. At 500 pcs/month, that's 75 scrapped parts — plus the cost of returns, delays, and emergency sourcing."

Our approach:
1. Analyzed the root cause (casting datum inconsistency)
2. Implemented dynamic work coordinate system
3. Added in-process probing for every part

Results after 3 months:
📊 Defect rate: 15% → 0.8%
📊 Per-part cost: -22% (less scrap = lower effective cost)
📊 Lead time: 25 days → 18 days

Sometimes the cheapest fix isn't changing suppliers — it's changing the process.

1. FICTION — ISO-certified Chinese shops match Western quality at 40-60% lower cost
2. FICTION — If your part can be machined in 2-3 setups on a 3-axis, 5-axis adds unnecessary cost
